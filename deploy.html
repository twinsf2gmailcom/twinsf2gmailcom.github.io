MOBY deployment Image Gallery

There are 2 buoys in rotation at the site. While one bouy is in the water the second buoy is being cleaned, repairs are done if needed, new lower batteries are installed and post deployment calibrations are done. The buoys are swaped out around every 4-5 months. During a normal deployment the new buoy is on the ship and when the ship arrives at the site the old buoy is disconnected from the mooring. And the new buoy is then put in the water. Divers attached it to the mooring, remove red bags on the arms, install the gershin tubes and remove all the cables needed for deployment. Then the old buoy is retrieved.

These photos show the deployment of the new buoy from a ship. The small boat then tows it to the mooring and connects the tether.
